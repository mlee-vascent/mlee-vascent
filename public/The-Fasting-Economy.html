Physiological Timeline of Fasting

Understanding the human body's metabolic shift from glucose reliance to fat oxidation and cellular repair. This timeline illustrates the biological milestones achieved during a prolonged fasting state.

⏱️ 0-4 Hours

Anabolic Phase

Blood sugar and insulin levels rise after the last meal. The body is focused on digestion, storing glycogen in the liver, and lipid synthesis.

📉 4-16 Hours

Catabolic Shift

Insulin drops drastically. The body exhausts liver glycogen stores and begins mobilizing fatty acids for energy. Glucagon levels rise.

🔥 16-24 Hours

Ketosis Onset

The liver begins converting stored fat into ketone bodies, which cross the blood-brain barrier to provide highly efficient fuel for the brain.

🧬 24+ Hours

Autophagy

Deep cellular repair begins. The body breaks down old, damaged proteins and misfolded cells to regenerate new, healthy cellular structures.

Economic Forces & Market Drivers

The "non-consumption" diet presents a unique economic paradox. It suppresses revenue in traditional food and snack sectors while creating a booming digital ecosystem of tracking applications and metabolic optimization supplements.

▲ Primary Market Drivers

  • Digital Health Adoption: Rise of tracking apps (Zero, Simple) offering premium subscriptions and personalized coaching.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Unlike expensive supplement-heavy diets, fasting is inherently free, increasing initial user adoption.
  • Corporate Wellness: Employers subsidizing fasting and metabolic health apps to reduce long-term healthcare premiums.

▼ Industry Barriers

  • Food Industry Pushback: Loss of revenue for the breakfast food and late-night snack market sectors.
  • Medical Skepticism: Slow adoption by traditional healthcare providers due to lack of patentable, monetizable pharmaceutical interventions.
  • User Churn Rate: High abandonment rate after 30 days due to lifestyle friction and social eating pressures.
